ABOUT THE LAURA FOLK FAIR

Celebrating community, culture, and connection for over four decades

OUR STORY

For over four decades, the Laura Folk Fair has been the heart of our community, bringing together families, friends, and neighbors in celebration of local culture, craftsmanship, and the enduring spirit of togetherness.

A JOURNEY THROUGH TIME

Humble Beginnings

The Laura Folk Fair was originally conceived by well known and respected local artist Richard 'Dick' Biles, who was also the founding President of the Rocky River Historic and Art Society (RRHAS) as well as teaching Art at the nearby Gladstone High School.

His vision was to promote art and culture in the local area and the first event included the unveiling of the bust of author CJ Dennis, which was commissioned by the RRHAS.

After the resounding success of the first event, the Laura Folk Fair saw tremendous growth and the event has since grown into one of the region's most beloved annual events, attracting visitors from far and wide.

A Modern Tradition

Today, the Laura Folk Fair welcomes over 15,000 visitors annually, features more than 100 stallholders, and has become the cornerstone of our community's cultural calendar.

We continue to evolve while staying true to our roots of celebrating local talent, fostering community connections, and creating lasting memories for families.

OUR MISSION

  • • To celebrate and preserve local culture, support community artisans and businesses, and create an inclusive space where people of all ages can come together to enjoy authentic experiences, build lasting connections, and strengthen the bonds that make our community special.
  • • Play a key role in sustaining a healthy, resilient community, while raising morale and supporting mental health. Encourage community cohesion, as well as a sense of pride in our town and district.
  • • Promote Laura to all who visit the area and increase tourism to our beautiful region.
  • • Raise funds, increase donations, create partnerships and continue to support other community groups and charitable organisations with their fundraising efforts.
  • • Grow the event and win awards for our efforts.
  • • Create memories that last a lifetime.

HONORING OUR FOUNDER

Richard 'Dick' Biles

Founder and Visionary (1980-2010?)

A passionate advocate for local artists, Dick's vision of bringing the community together through celebration of local culture became the foundation of our fair. His legacy lives on in every stall, every smile, and every connection made at our event.
